# PRAGMA Administrative Board – Quarterly Report  
## Q1 2026 (October–December)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Q1 2026 focused on operationalizing the Project Committee established in Q4 2025, addressing outstanding maintainer 
committee compliance, resolving a trademark opposition and defining PRAGMA's financial footing through membership 
dues. 

Several items carried forward from Q4 2025 were progressed or resolved, including approval of IP settlement 
concessions, a decision to reduce reliance on external legal retainer and preparation for the Annual General 
Assembly scheduled for May 2026.

## 3. Project Committee (Phase 2)

Status: Operational
The Project Committee, elected in Q4 2025, held its inaugural meeting in January. The board reviewed the PDR-11
mandate and briefed the committee on the roles of chair and secretary. The committee was reminded that:

- Projects must be open source
- In the first year, projects must not originate from founding businesses, to ensure diversity and fairness
- The committee's scope is currently limited to the Cardano ecosystem, with potential future expansion

The committee developed a Google Form application process for prospective projects, with the Board updating 
PDR-3 to reflect this approach rather than requiring applicants to create a PDR directly. The application 
form was targeted for finalisation by end of February, anticipating applications opening to prospective projects in 
in March and a proposed four-week application period.

The projectcommittee@pragma.io email account was set up and made operational. Distribution of messaging and the 
application form to the community was reviewed in March. Committee member Geoff Watson was inactive 
during March and expected to resume participation in April.

## 4. Maintainer Committees

The board tracked compliance from both Aiken and Amaru maintainer committees throughout the quarter.
Amaru submitted a partial "ways of working" operations document in January, which was added to the board's shared 
drive for review. The Amaru Q4 report was retrieved from Amaru's website.

Aiken remained non-compliant throughout the quarter: quarterly reports and operations documentation were not 
submitted. The board discussed the need to update PDR-0008 with a defined process for addressing unresponsive 
leads, including possible replacement and escalation steps. The Aiken Maintainer Committee lead is to be 
contacted formally regarding outstanding obligations.

## 5. Financial & Accounting Updates

Bank access transition was completed during the quarter, with Benjamin Bürgi confirmed as co-approver for 
invoice payments. Bank statements are being prepared by the external accounting provider to finalise 2025 
accounts.

A proposed annual membership fee of CHF 8,900 per member organisation was reviewed by the board in February as 
a reasonable baseline estimate, with acknowledgment that adjustments may be needed depending on member uptake. 
The board noted a risk that some member businesses may decline payment and requested a contingency approach be 
developed.

The board discussed reducing the management and housekeeping budget given the changes to legal support 
(see Section 7). Trademark and IP protection remains the largest budget item and requires further definition, 
including exploration of tiered trademark services based on project maturity and more cost-effective automated 
monitoring options.

## 6. Registry & Credential Management

The board discussed PRAGMA's approach to administrative ownership of project assets, including GitHub 
repositories and social media accounts. The board expressed a preference for maintaining organisational 
control with the ability to delegate access, rather than relying solely on trust-based arrangements.
Two credential management tools were evaluated:

- Bitwarden (open source): modest fee, positive board experience reported
- 1Password (proprietary): higher fee, negative experience reported

A recommendation with cost comparison is to be provided at the next GA meeting for further input or decisions.

## 7. Legal & Brand Matters

### Trademark Opposition

An update on the ongoing trademark opposition was reviewed in January. Legal counsel's recommendations were 
accepted as largely straightforward. A proposed settlement and coexistence agreement was discussed, including 
the question of conceding protection under Class 9 (hardware devices), which counsel advised is not essential 
to PRAGMA's software-focused mission.

In March, updated settlement correspondence was reviewed. Open legal questions remained, and a direct call 
with counsel was requested to clarify constraints before a final board decision.

### Legal Retainer

In January, the board determined that PRAGMA may best benefit from a combination of a reduced legal retainer 
and utilizing the legal skills of CF's board representative.

## 8. General Assembly

The Annual General Assembly was confirmed for Wednesday, 6 May 2026 at 10:00 AM Eastern Time. The notice and 
invitation has been in accordance with statutes, assisted by Probst.

The board discussed whether the Annual General Assembly could be conducted virtually. Legal confirmation 
from counsel that a virtual meeting is permissible under PRAGMA's governing rules was requested before 
proceeding on that basis.

An open action item from the prior General Assembly — a request relating to format categories for open 
coding sessions — was noted for follow-up to seek clarity before the next meeting.

The board agreed that a GA quarterly meeting should be held in April rather than being grouped into the 
May Annual meeting. April's board meeting is also to include GA agenda preparation.

## 9. Communication Practices

The board reviewed communication consistency and response gaps. To reduce channel fragmentation and 
establish clearer paths for board-level feedback, the board voted to remove the legacy 
communication server and transition to direct communication channels. The decision needs to be vetted by Legal
council before proceeding with removal.
